The access to medicines is a fundamental component of the full realization of the right to health. Medical care in case of illness and prevention, as well as treatment and control of diseases, depend to a large extent on timely and adequate access to quality medicines. access to medicines is intrinsically linked to the principles of equality and non-discrimination, transparency, participation and responsibility. There remains an intrinsic link between poverty and the realization of the right to health, with developing countries having the greatest need and least access to medicines.
